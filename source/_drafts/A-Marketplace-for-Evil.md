I am a huge proponent of "Never confuse the map for the territory"

This one statement captures so much of modern life. We the poeple, humans by and large, are incredbly adept at making mental maps.

We live off the mental map. If you think about real maps, they have an incredible power over us. Especially mapping services that provide traffic analysis aimed to help you get around. It is so easy to confuse the real roads for the map.

Not so you say. I understand that my car drives on concrete road and not on a digital backlit-screen. My counter might be "Tell me about your selection process of which concrete road to drive on"

Often it takes a big effort to be the fish who thinks about what it's like to live in water. To put some real life on these abstract concepts.
